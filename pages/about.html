Who I Am

My obsession with technology started early—not with high-level code, but with a screwdriver and 90s computer peripherals. Whether it was dismantling hardware or building animations in Flash 5, I was always the kid who needed to know how things worked under the hood. That curiosity eventually turned into a career. From my early days in Bengaluru to completing my Master's at McMaster University, my focus has remained the same: solving real-world problems by mastering the fundamentals rather than just chasing the latest industry jargon.

In my professional life, I've always believed in leading through action. During my 3.5 years at National Instruments (now Emerson), I worked my way up from an intern to a Staff Software Engineer. Being named "Rookie of the Year" was a highlight, but the real reward was the "impossible" problems we solved along the way. I thrive in environments that require high ownership and resilience, and I take pride in building systems that aren't just functional, but architecturally sound and scalable.

Lately, I've been diving deep into the intersection of AI, Distributed Systems, and Core Algorithms. I'm a firm believer that the best engineers are the ones who understand the logic behind the tools. This mindset led me to master the NeetCode 150 and eventually develop and publish my own string algorithms. For me, there's a unique thrill in optimizing a system at the algorithmic level—moving beyond existing solutions to create something more efficient.

Outside of work, I’m usually exploring interests that keep my curiosity sharp. I enjoy experimenting with new recipes in the kitchen, and I’ve always been drawn to subjects like Literature, Astronomy, Philosophy, and Psychology. I find that having a broad perspective often helps me approach engineering problems with more creativity. Now that I’ve finished my Master’s, I’m looking for my next opportunity to tackle complex challenges with a team that values deep technical work and solid fundamentals.
